Frequently Asked Questions

To fix a leaking kitchen faucet, start by turning off the water supply. Then, disassemble the faucet to identify the worn-out parts, such as washers or O-rings, and replace them. Reassemble the faucet and turn the water back on to check for leaks.

A slow-draining kitchen sink is usually caused by a clog in the pipes. Remove any visible debris and try using a plunger to dislodge the blockage. If that doesn't work, use a plumbing snake or a mixture of baking soda and vinegar to clear the pipes.

A foul smell in the kitchen sink often results from food particles or grease trapped in the drain. Clean the sink trap by removing and cleaning it thoroughly. You can also pour a mixture of baking soda followed by vinegar down the drain, let it sit, and then flush with hot water.

To prevent clogs, avoid putting grease, coffee grounds, or fibrous foods down the drain. Use a sink strainer to catch food particles. Regularly flush the drain with hot water or a mild baking soda and vinegar solution to help dissolve any accumulating gunk.

To prevent your garbage disposal from jamming, always run cold water while using it. Avoid grinding pork chops, chicken bones, or fibrous fruits and vegetables. If it jams, use an Allen wrench to manually turn the disposal motor and free the blockage, or press the reset button on the unit.
