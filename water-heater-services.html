Frequently Asked Questions

It is recommended to have your water heater serviced at least once a year to ensure it operates efficiently and lasts longer.

Common signs include inconsistent water temperature, unusual noises, leakage, rusty or discolored water, and reduced hot water supply.

Yes, you can flush your water heater yourself. It should be done at least once a year to remove sediment build-up and improve efficiency.

The main types of water heaters are tankless (on-demand), storage tank, heat pump, solar, and condensing water heaters. Each type has its own advantages depending on your needs and location.

To improve efficiency, consider insulating your water heater and pipes, lowering the thermostat setting, and performing regular maintenance checks.
