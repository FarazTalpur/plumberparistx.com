Frequently Asked Questions

You typically need a drill, adjustable wrench, tubing cutter, and a water supply line kit that includes necessary fittings and connectors.

Yes, you can install an ice maker line yourself if you have basic plumbing skills. However, ensure you are comfortable working with home plumbing connections.

Turn off the water supply, attach the water line to the shut-off valve, run it to the refrigerator, and connect it to the ice maker inlet following the manufacturer’s instructions.

Ensure all connections are tight, use Teflon tape on threaded connections, and check for leaks by slowly turning the water back on and inspecting the connections.

Most ice makers use a 1/4-inch supply line size, but always refer to your ice maker's manual for the correct size and specifications.
