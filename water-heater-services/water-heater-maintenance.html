Frequently Asked Questions

It's recommended to flush your water heater at least once a year to remove sediment build-up and ensure its efficiency and longevity.

The ideal temperature setting for a water heater is typically around 120Â°F (49Â°C) to prevent scalding and improve energy efficiency.

Rumbling or popping noises usually indicate sediment build-up at the bottom of the tank. Regular flushing can help prevent this noise by clearing out the sediment.

Signs include inconsistent water temperatures, strange noises, rusty water, leaks, and reduced hot water supply. If you notice any of these, it's time to perform maintenance or consult a professional.

To improve efficiency, insulate the water heater tank and pipes, regularly flush the tank, lower the temperature setting, and consider installing a timer for electric units to control when it heats water.
